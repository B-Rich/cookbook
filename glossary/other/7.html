The seventh counting number, the fourth prime number, the first hexahedral number. It is the third Mersenne number and the second Mersenne prime. It has the property that 7!+1 is a square (only 4,5 and 7 have this property). Its Fermat quotient is a square (the only other number for which this is the case is 3). It is the third Lucky number. 7 colours are sufficient to colour any map on a torus. ϕ(7) = 6,  d(7) = 2,  σ(7) = 8.
