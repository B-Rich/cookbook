Given two co-ordinate systems xi and $ \bar{\mathrm{x}}^{\mathrm{i}} , $ a mixed tensor of order 2 is a set of components denoted Aji (with respect to xi) or $ \bar{\mathrm{A}}^{\mathrm{i}} _{\mathrm{j}} $ (with respect to $ \bar{\mathrm{x}}^{\mathrm{i}} ) , $ and satisfying:
$$\bar{\mathrm{A}}^{\mathrm{i}} _{\mathrm{j}} = \sum _{\mathrm{r}} \sum _{\mathrm{s}} \frac{\partial \bar{\mathrm{x}}^{\mathrm{i}}} {\partial \mathrm{x}^{\mathrm{r}}} \frac{\partial \mathrm{x}^{\mathrm{s}}} {\partial \bar{\mathrm{x}}^{\mathrm{j}}} \mathrm{A}^{\mathrm{r}} _{\mathrm{s}} ,$$
for every i and j.

Higher-order mixed tensors can be defined similarly. Note that unlike covariant or contravariant tensors, a mixed tensor has both superscripts and subscripts.
