If X ∼ B(n, p), i.e. X is distributed binomially with parameters n and p, then:
$$\langle X \rangle = \sum _{r=0} ^{n} rp_{r} = \sum _{r=0} ^{n} r {n \choose r} p ^ {r} (1- p ) ^ { n-r}$$

$$= \sum _{r=1} ^{n} \frac{n!}{(r-1)!(n-r)!} p^{r} (1-p)^{n-r}$$

$$= np \sum _{r=1} ^ {n} \frac{(n-1)!}{(r-1!)(n-r)!} p^{r-1} (1-p) ^ {n-r}$$

$$= np \sum _{r=0} ^ { n-1} {{n-1} \choose r} p^{r} (1-p)^ {n-1-r}$$

 = np(p + (1 − p))n − 1
