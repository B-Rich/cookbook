A group which is generated by a single element is called cyclic. For instance, the group of rotations through multiples of 90 ∘  can be generated from the single element which is a single clockwise rotation of 90 ∘ . The other rotations are produced by repeating this rotation.

The single element is called a generator for the group.

A cyclic group of order n is isomorphic to the group of integers modulo n under addition.
