Sometimes, a difficult integral can be integrated by parts several times, yielding either successively simpler inetgrals or one that can be related to the original. The process iterates towards a solution. For example:


$$\begin{aligned} \mathrm{I} &=& \int \mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{x}} \mathrm{\cos x dx} \\ &=& \mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{x}}\mathrm{\sin x} - \int \mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{x}}\mathrm{\sin x dx} \\ &=& \mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{x}}\mathrm{\sin x} + \mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{x}}\mathrm{\cos x } - \int \mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{x}}\mathrm{\cos x dx} \\ &=& \mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{x}}(\mathrm{\sin x + cos x}) - \mathrm{I} \\ 2 \mathrm{I} &=& \mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{x}}(\mathrm{\sin x + \cos x}) \\ \mathrm{I} &=& \frac{\mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{x}}}{2}(\mathrm{\sin x + \cos x})\end{aligned}$$
