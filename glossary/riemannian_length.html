In a space with n dimensions, x1, x2, ...xi,  a Riemannian length function of the form:
∑gij(x)dx ixj
may be defined and used instead of the normal Pythagorean distance function. This gives a Riemannian geometry, which has properties like those of curved space in a gravitational field. Euclid’s fifth axiom does not hold; nor does the second axiom, which says that a line may be extended indefinitely in either direction.
