A line and a plane which are not parallel intersect in a point.

If the equations of the line are x = pz + d, y = qz + e, and the equation of the plane is z = mx + ny + c, then the point of intersection is at:


$$\begin{aligned} x&=&\frac{d-nqd+nep+cp}{1-mp-nq}\\ y&=&\frac{e-mpe+mdq+cq}{1-mp-nq}\\ z&=&\frac{md+ne+c}{1-mp-nq}.\end{aligned}$$
