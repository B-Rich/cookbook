An operation is associative if, when the operation appears twice in an expression, it doesn’t matter where you put the brackets.

For instance, addition is associative because
(a + b) + c ≡ a + (b + c)
This is called the associative law. Multiplication is associative because
(ab)c ≡ a(bc)
Division and subtraction are not associative since
$$(a-b)-c \not \equiv a - (b-c)$$
and
$$\frac{\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)}{c} \not \equiv \frac{a}{\left(\frac{b}{c}\right)}$$
