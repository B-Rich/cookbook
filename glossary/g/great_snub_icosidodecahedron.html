The 62nd uniform polyhedron is the Great snub icosidodecahedron; it has Wythoff symbol |2 5/2 3. Its dual is the great pentagonal hexecontahedron. The pattern of polygons round each vertex is (3,3,5/2,3,3); its symmetry group is the icosahedral group A5.

It has:

Euler characteristic: 2

Density: 7

Number of vertices: 60

Number of edges: 150

Number of faces: 92 (=80 triangles and 125/2).

Note: a polygon a/b means a shape made by taking a points and joining every bth of them; for instance 5/2 means a five-pointed star made by taking five points and joining every second point.
