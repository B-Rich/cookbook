Let D be a domain and z0 ∈ D . Then if f : D \ z0 → C is holomorphic and f is bounded near z0 then there is a number a such that f(z) → a as z → z0 . Moreover, if we define g : D → C by
$$g(z)= \left \{ \begin{array}{cc} f(z) & z \neq z_0 \\ a & z=z_0, \end{array} \right .$$
then g is holomorphic and we have ‘removed’ the singularity at z0 .
