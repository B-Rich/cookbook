A group with a commutative operation; for example, all cyclic groups are Abelian. Note that additive notation is often used in place of the more usual group multiplication, so that g.h becomes g + h , and ak becomes ka (where k ∈ Z ). In fact, every Abelian group is isomorphic to a group of the form
Zk ⊕ Z/d1Z ⊕ … ⊕ Z/dnZ
where d1∣…∣dn . This is equivalent to the Chinese remainder theorem (possibly only for finite groups).
