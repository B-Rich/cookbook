A topological space (X, τ) is connected if we cannot find open disjoint non-empty subsets U, V ⊆ X such that X = U ∪ V . Such sets U, V would be said to disconnect X . Equivalent conditions to connectedness are that every continuous function X ↦ Z is constant, and that the only subsets that are both open and closed are ∅ and X itself. The connected subsets of R are precisely the intervals. Compare Hausdorff space, path connected, polygonally connected.
