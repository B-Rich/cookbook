A kind of oscillation for which the equation of motion is $ \frac{d^2 x}{dt^2} + \omega ^2 x = 0 . $

It occurs when a system has an equilibrium position, is subject to a restoring force proportional to the size of the displacement from equilibrium, and is capable of ’overshooting’ beyond the equilibrium position. The position of the system varies sinusoidally over time, with constant amplitude.

ω is called the angular frequency.

The equation of motion is solved by x = Asin(ωt) + Bcos(ωt),  or by x = asin(ωt + ε),  where $ a = \sqrt{A^2 + B^2 } $ and sinε = B/a, cosε = A/a.

Also, v2 = ω2(a2 − x2),  and the period T of the motion is given by $ T = \frac{ 2 \pi }{ \omega} . $
