Let V and W be vector spaces over a field F. A bilinear form on V × W is a function φ : V × W → F which is linear in each variable separately, i.e. restricting to a single point in either vector space we obtain a linear map in the other variable.

For example, if V = W = Fn then the map φ(v, w) = ∑i = 1nviwi is a bilinear form.

Given bases v1, …, vn for V and w1, …, wn for W , we associate a matrix A with bilinear form φ by setting Aij = φ(vi, wj) . This means that φ(x, y) may be calculated as
φ(x, y) = ∑i = 1n∑j = 1mxiyjAij = xTAb.
We can always find bases for V and W such that the matrix of φ is of the form
$$\left( \begin{array}{cc} I_r & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{array} \right),$$
where Ir is the r × r identity matrix.
