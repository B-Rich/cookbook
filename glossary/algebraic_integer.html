A subset of a number field K/Q that plays a role similar to that played by Z in Q . An element a of K is an algebraic integer if there is a monic polynomial f(X) ∈ Z[X] such that f(a) = 0 . The set of such numbers is written OK ; an equivalent characterisation of the property a ∈ OK is that the NMP of a lies in Z[X] . Also, an algebraic integer has norm and trace in Z . The “usual integers” Z may be referred to as rational integers, as Z = OQ . The set of algebraic integers OK is a subring of K , and also if β is a root of a monic polynomial with coefficients in OK , then β is also an algebraic integer. We may always find an integral basis for OK , i.e. a set αi of algebraic integers such that
OK = Zα1 + … + Zαn, 
where n = [K : Q] . For example, for the quadratic field $ K= \mathbf{Q}( \sqrt{d}) $ , d ∈ Z \ 0, 1 square free, we have
