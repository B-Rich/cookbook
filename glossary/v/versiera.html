Take a circle with a diameter PQ, and draw a tangent to the circle at Q. Pick a point R on the circle, and extend the line PR to intersect the tangent. Call this intersection S. Draw a line parallel to PQ through S, and one parallel to the tangent through R. The intersection of these last two lines is called P’. The locus of P’ as R moves around the circle, is called a versiera or witch of Agnesi.

The curve has an equation of the form: xy2 = 4a2(2a − x),  where a is the radius of the circle.
