$$\lim_{ \mathrm{x} \to \mathrm{a}} \frac {\mathrm{f(x)}}{\mathrm{g(x)}} = \frac{\displaystyle{\lim_{\mathrm{x} \to \mathrm{a}}}\frac{\mathrm{df}}{\mathrm{dx}}} {\displaystyle{\lim_{\mathrm{x} \to \mathrm{a}}}\frac{\mathrm{dg}}{\mathrm{dx}}}$$

This is a useful rule for finding the limit of a ratio, if both parts of the ratio go to zero at the limit;

eg,
$$\lim_{\mathrm{x} \to 1} \frac {\mathrm{x}^2 - 1}{\mathrm{x}^2 - 3 \mathrm{x} + 2} = \frac{\displaystyle{\lim_{\mathrm{x} \to 1}}2 \mathrm{x}} {\displaystyle{\lim_{\mathrm{x} \to 1}}2 \mathrm{x} - 3}$$

$$= \frac{2}{-1}$$

 =  − 2

This is known as l’Hôpital’s rule.
