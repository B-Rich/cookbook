A measure of how asymmetric a distribution is. There are several such measures, but they share the feature that a long tail on the right is called positive skewness and a long tail on the left is called negative skewness. Skewness attempts to measure how much of the ’weight’ of the distribution lies to the right of the mean.

The coefficient of skewness is γ1 = μ3/μ23/2,  where μi is the ith moment about the mean.

Other measures of skewness are:

(mean-mode)/(standard deviation);


$$\frac{Q_3 - 2Q_2 + Q_1 }{Q_3 - Q_1} ,$$
where Q1,  Q2andQ3 are the first quartile, median and third quartile respectively.
