If A is a symmetric positive definite matrix, we can write A = RTR where R is an upper triangular matrix with positive entries on the leading diagonal. This is called a Cholesky factorisation of A.

For example,
$$\left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & -2 \\ -2 & 13 \end{array} \right) = \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0 \\ -2 & 3 \end{array} \right) \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 & -2 \\ 0 & 3 \end{array} \right).$$
