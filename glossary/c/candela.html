A unit of luminous intensity, symbol cd.

The luminance or photometric brightness of a black body at the temperature of freezing platinum equals 60 candela per square centimetre.

So, the candela is the luminous intensity (in the perpendicular direction) of a surface of 1/600 000 m2 of a black body at the temperature of freezing platinum under a pressure of 101 325 Nm − 2.
