If four quantities a, b, c, d satisfy a:b=c:d, then we say that they have a common ratio. This is the case, for instance, if a and b are two lengths in a shape, and c and d are the corresponding lengths in an enlargement of the shape.

In a geometric series a, ar, ar2, ar3,  ..., r is the common ratio, as the ratio of each term to the previous one is r.
