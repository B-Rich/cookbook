There are lots of ways to calculate π; for instance

$ \frac{\pi}{4} = 1 - (1/3) + (1/5) - (1/7) + ... $ (Leibnitz’s series)

$ \frac{\pi}{2} = \frac{2}{1} \cdot \frac{2}{3} \cdot \frac{4}{3} \cdot \frac{4}{5} \cdot \frac{6}{5} \cdot \frac{6}{7} \cdot \frac{8}{7} \cdot \frac{8}{9} ... $ (Walliss formula)
