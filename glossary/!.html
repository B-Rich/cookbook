n! means n × (n − 1) × (n − 2) × … × 2 × 1. This is called n factorial or the factorial of n. It becomes very large as n gets bigger. Note that we take 0! = 1.


$$\begin{aligned} 0! & = & 1;\\ 1! & = & 1;\\ 2! & = & 2;\\ 3! & = & 6;\\ 4! & = & 24;\\ 5! & = & 120;\\ 6! & = & 720; \end{aligned}$$

etc.
