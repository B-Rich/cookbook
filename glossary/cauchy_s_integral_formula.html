Let D be a domain and f : D → C be holomorphic. Writing Cr[z0] : [0, 1] → C as the path defined by Cr[z0](t) = z0 + re2πt , then if the track of Cr[z0] and z0 itself lie entirely within D , then
$$f(z)= \frac{1}{2 \pi i} \int_{C_r[z_0]} \frac{f(w)}{w-z} dz.$$
This generalises to show that holomorphy implies that f is infinitely differentiable on D , with
$$f^{(n)}(z)= \frac{n!}{2 \pi i} \int_{C_r[z_0]} \frac{f(w)}{(w-z)^{n+1}} dw.$$
