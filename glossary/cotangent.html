In a right-angled triangle, if one of the angles is θ, then the cotangent of θ is the length of the side next to θ divided by the length of the side opposite θ.
$$\cot \theta = \frac{\mbox{adjacent}}{\mbox{opposite}}$$
More generally, $cot \theta = \frac{x}{y}$, where (x, y) is the point obtained by rotating (1, 0) about (0, 0) through the angle θ.
$$\cot \theta = \frac{1}{\tan \theta} = \frac{cos \theta}{sin \theta}.$$
