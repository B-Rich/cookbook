Sometimes we are interested in how widely a random variable is spread about its mean value. Two ways to measure this are the variance and the standard deviation.

We could try to use the average value of X − ⟨X⟩,  but we find that this is always zero:
⟨X − ⟨X⟩⟩ = ∑i=1npi(xi − ⟨X⟩)

 = ∑i=1npixi − ∑i=1npi⟨X⟩ = ⟨X⟩ − ⟨X⟩ = 0

Instead we use measures of spread such as the average squared difference between X and its mean.
