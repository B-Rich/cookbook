The standard error of a sample statistic is the standard deviation of the difference between that statistic and the corresponding population parameter. For example, if the statistic t is being used to estimate the parameter τ,  the standard error of t is the standard deviation of t − τ.

Unless we say otherwise, we are usually talking about the standard error of the sample mean, which for a sample of size n is $ \sigma / \sqrt{n} , $ where σ is the population standard deviation.

Confusingly, the estimated standard error is sometimes also called the ’standard error’.
