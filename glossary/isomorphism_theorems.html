First isomorphism theorem: Suppose θ : G → H is a group homomorphism. Then kerθ ⊲ G and there is a group isomorphism θ̄ : G/kerθ → Im(θ) so that θ factors as
$$G \to G/ ker \theta \overset{\bar{\theta}}{\longrightarrow} Im(\theta) \hookrightarrow H.$$
In particular, note that G/kerθ ≅ Im(θ) .

Second isomorphism theorem: Suppose H ≤ G and K ⊲ G . Then HK ≤ G ; also K ⊲ HK and H ∩ G = ∅ . What is more,
H/(H ∩ K) ≅ HK/K.

Third isomorphism theorem: Suppose that N ⊲ G . Then H ↦ H/N is a bijection between subgroups H with N ≤ H ≤ G and the subgroups of G/N . Under this bijection, H ⊲ G iff h/N ⊲ G/N and so
$$\frac{G/N}{H/N} \cong G/H.$$
