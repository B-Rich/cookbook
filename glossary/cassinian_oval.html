Given two points A and B and a constant c2,  the locus of points P with PA.PB = c2 is called Cassini’s ovals.

The locus has equation (x2 + y2)2 − 2a2(x2 − y2)2 − a4 + c4 = 0,  where a=AB.

This is the same set of curves as the cross-sections of a circular torus taken parallel to its axis.

If c=a, then the curve is a lemniscate.
