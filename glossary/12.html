Twelve is the first number which can be written as a product of its proper divisors in more than one way: 12 = 2 ⋅ 6 = 3 ⋅ 4.

It is also the first abundant number.

It is the number of edges of both the cube and the octahedron.

It is the first abundant number and therefore the first superabundant number.

It is the number of faces of the dodecahedron, a Platonic solid, and the number of vertices of the icosahedron.

It is the third pentagonal number.

ϕ(12) = 4,  d(12) = 6,  σ(12) = 28.
