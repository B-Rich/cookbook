For a first order differential equation $ \frac{dy}{dx} + Py = Q , $ where P and Q are functions of x, an integrating factor is R = e∫Pdx. Note that $ \frac{dR}{dx} = RP , $ so $ \frac{d}{dx}(Ry) = R \frac{dy}{dx} +RPy . $

So if we multiply the original equation by R, we have

$ R \frac{dy}{dx} + RPy = RQ , $

so, $ \frac{d}{dx} (Ry) = RQ, $

so, Ry = ∫RQdx,  which gives us solutions.
