If f1(x), f2(x)...fn(x) are all functions having continuous derivatives up to the (n − 1)th derivative, then their Wronskian is:
$$\mathrm{W(x)} = \left | \begin{array}{cccc} \mathrm{f}_1 \mathrm{(x)} & \mathrm{f}_2 \mathrm{(x)} & ... & \mathrm{f}_{\mathrm{n}} \mathrm{(x)} \\ \mathrm{f} ^{\prime} _1 \mathrm{(x)} & \mathrm{f} ^{\prime} _2 \mathrm{(x)} & ... & \mathrm{f} ^{\prime} _{\mathrm{n}} \mathrm{(x)} \\ \vdots & \vdots & & \vdots \\ \mathrm{f}_1 ^{\mathrm{(n-1)}} \mathrm{(x)} & \mathrm{f}_2 ^{\mathrm{(n-1)}} \mathrm{(x)} & ... & \mathrm{f}_{\mathrm{n}} ^{\mathrm{(n-1)}} \mathrm{(x)} \end{array} \right |$$
