In linear maths: A map τ : V → W between vector spaces satisfying

τ(v + w) = τ(v) + τ(w)

τ(λv) = λτ(v)

or equivalently just τ(λv + μw) = λτ(v) + μτ(w) , for all scalars λ, μ and all v, w ∈ V . Note that V and W must be defined over the same field F for this to make any sense. If V = W , τ is called an endomorphism.

Every linear map between finite-dimensional vector spaces corresponds to a matrix, given an isomorphism between the vector spaces and Fn . This is done by setting the j th column of the matrix to be the image of the i th basis vector of Fn ≅ V . The kernel of τ is the set of all values taken by τ to the zero element of W . This is clearly a subspace of V , and similarly the image τ(V) is a subspace of W . The rank rkτ of τ is the dimension of its image space (i.e. the set τ(V) ⊆ W ). The nullity nullτ is the dimension of the kernel; the rank-nullity formula
rkτ + nullτ = dimV
relates these quantities. See also n-linear, isomorphism, Jordan normal form, minimum polynomial, characteristic polynomial, nilpotent
