In complex analysis, a function ϕ : C ↦ C, ϕ(x, y) = u(x, y) + iv(x, y) is analytic, or complex differentiable if its real and imaginary parts u and v satisfy the Cauchy-Riemann equations:


$$\begin{aligned} \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} &=\frac{\partial v}{\partial y}\\ \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} &=-\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}\end{aligned}$$

Functions u and v for which these equations hold true are known as conjugate functions.
