For given m × n matrix A, we can always find orthogonal matrices U (m × m) and V (n × n) such that
$$U^T A V = \Sigma = \left ( \begin{array}{ccccc} \sigma_1 & \ldots & & & \\ \vdots & \sigma_2 & \ldots & & \\ & & \ddots & & \\ & & & \sigma_p \end{array} \right )$$
where p=min(m,n). The values σi are called the singular values of A.
