The symbol Σ is used to denote the sum of the term or terms that follow it, taken over the range given above and below the symbol. For example, ∑i = 0ni2 means the sum of i2 for all values of i from 0 to n, i.e. 02 + 12 + ... + n2.

If a range for the variable is not given, we sum over all possible values of it: $ \sum _{i} \frac{1}{i^2} $ means the sum of $ \frac{1}{i^2} $ for every integer i.
