The adjoint of a matrix is the transpose of the matrix made up of the cofactors of the original matrix.

So,
$$\mathrm{adj} \left ( \begin{array}{ccc} \mathrm{a} & \mathrm{b} & \mathrm{c} \\ \mathrm{d} & \mathrm{e} & \mathrm{f} \\ \mathrm{g} & \mathrm{h} & \mathrm{i} \end{array} \right ) = \left ( \begin{array}{ccc} \mathrm{(ei - hf)} & \mathrm{(hc - bi)} & \mathrm{(bf - ec)} \\ \mathrm{(gf - di)} & \mathrm{(ai - gc)} & \mathrm{(dc - af)} \\ \mathrm{(dh - ge)} & \mathrm{(gb - ah)} & \mathrm{(ae - db)} \end{array} \right )$$
