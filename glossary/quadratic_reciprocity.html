The theorem, due to Gauss, stating that, for p and q odd primes,
$$\left ( \frac{p}{q} \right ) \left ( \frac{q}{p} \right ) =(-1)^{ ( \frac{p-1}{2} \frac{q-1}{2} ) } \left \{ \begin{array}{cc} 1 & p \quad\mbox{or}\quad q \equiv 1 (mod 4) \\ -1 & p \quad \mbox{and}\quad q \equiv 3 (mod 4) \end{array} \right .$$

See Legendre symbol.
