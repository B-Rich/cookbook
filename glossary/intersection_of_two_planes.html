Two planes that are not parallel intersect in a line.

If the two planes have normals m and n, their vector equations are m.r = c and n.r = d for c, d ∈ R. The angle α between the two planes is the same as the angle between their normals, and can be calculated using from m.n = ∣m∣∣n∣cosα.

The cross product of the normals m × n gives a vector at right angles to both and therefore in the direction of the line of intersection of the planes.

Choosing a point a lying on the line of intersection, we can then write down the equation of this line:
r = a + tm × n where t ∈ R
