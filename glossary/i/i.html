The first imaginary number; it is defined by saying that i2 =  − 1. Note that because i does not lie on the real number line, it is neither greater than nor less than 1. i⃗ is also used to denote the unit vector in the x direction: $ x = \left( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array} \right ) $ in two dimensions or $ x = \left ( \begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{array} \right ) $ in three dimensions. Capital I is used to denote the identity mapping, or the identity matrix.
