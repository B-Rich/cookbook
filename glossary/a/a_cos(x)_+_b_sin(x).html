acos(x) + bsin(x) is a linear combination of cos(x) and sin(x). Since acos(x) + bsin(x) = rcos(x − t) where $r= \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$ and $\cos(t)=\frac{a}{r}$, $sin(t)=\frac{b}{r}$, this linear combination can always be expressed as a simple cosine (or sine) with a certain amplitude r and phase t. Also, acos(x) + bsin(x) = rsin(x + u) where $ r= \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} $ and $\sin(u)=\frac{a}{r}, cos(u)=\frac{b}{r}$.
