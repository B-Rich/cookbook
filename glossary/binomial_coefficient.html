We write $ {p \choose q} $ to mean $ \frac{p!}{q! (p-q)!} $ . It is the same as pCq,  or ${p \choose q}$. A number like this is called a binomial coefficient, because they occur in the binomial expansion, eg:
$$(x + y)^4 = {4 \choose 0} x^4 + {4 \choose 1} x^3 y+ {4 \choose 2} x^2 y^2 + {4 \choose 3} xy^3 + {4 \choose 4} y^4$$

 = x4 + 4x3y + 6x2y2 + 4xy3 + y4.

Also, the binomial coefficient $ {p \choose q} $ is equal to the number in the pth row, q places from the left, in Pascal’s triangle (starting to count at zero).
