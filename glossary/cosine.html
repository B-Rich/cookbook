In a right-angled triangle, if one of the angles is θ, then the cosine of θ is the length of the side adjacent to θ, divided by the length of the hypotenuse.
$$\cos \theta = \frac{\mbox{adjacent}}{\mbox{hypoteneuse}}$$
Alternatively, and more generally, cosθ is the x coordinate of a point P obtained by rotating (1, 0) about the origin through an angle θ.
