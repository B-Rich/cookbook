A damped harmonic system $ \frac{d^2 x}{dt^2} + \alpha \frac{dx}{dt} + \omega ^2 x = 0 $ is described as overdamped if α > 2ω.

In this case the equation is solved by x = Aemt + Bent where m and n are the roots of the auxiliary equation.

The system does not oscillate at all; it simply returns to the equilibrium position and stays there.
