An elastic body under tension obeys the law that the force exerted is proportional to the extension of the body: T = kx, where T is the tension, x the extension and k the stiffness of the body.

Alternatively, the force exerted is proportional the the proportional extension of the body:

$ T = \lambda \frac{x}{l_0} , $ where T is the tension, x the extension, l0 the initial length and λ the modulus of elasticity.
