Let (X, τ) and (Y, σ) be topological spaces. Then the product topology on X × Y is the collection of all possible unions of sets of the form U × V where U ∈ τ and V ∈ σ . This has the obvious extension to the product topology on the Cartesian product of n topological spaces. For example, the product topology on R × R where R is R with the usual topology, is the same as the usual topology on R2 .
