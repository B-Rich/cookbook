Luckily there are non-trivial test functions, for example the bump function defined g by
g(x) = ∫ − ∞xh(t + α) − h(t − α)dt
where h(x) = 0 outside ( − η, η) for some η < α and is constructed by considering the test function
$$E(t)= \left \{ \begin{array}{cc} e^{-1/t^2} & t>0 \\ 0 & \mbox{otherwise} \end{array} \right .$$
and G(t) = E(t)E(1 − t) ∈ D for at + b = x . Then g ∈ D is constant on ( − α + η, α − η) and zero outside of ∣x∣ < α + η . We may equip D with a notion of convergence: suppose that fj is a sequence in D and f ∈ D . Then we say that
$$f_j \underset{ \mathcal{D}}{ \to} F$$
